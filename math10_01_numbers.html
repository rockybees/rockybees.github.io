Number Systems

\( \textbf{Natural Numbers} \ \mathbb{N} \): \( \{1, 2, 3, ...\} \)

\( \textbf{Whole Numbers} \ \mathbb{W} \): \( \{0, 1, 2, 3, ...\} \)

\( \textbf{Integers} \ \mathbb{Z} \): \( \{..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...\} \)

\( \textbf{Rational numbers} \) \( \mathbb{Q} \): All the numbers that can be expressed as a fraction of two integers, i.e., the quotient of two integers with a non-zero denominator. E.g., \( -9, -3/2, 0, 1.\overline{78}, 59/33, 2.33, 4. \) In mathematical notation:

\[ \mathbb{Q} = \left\{ \frac{a}{b} \mid a, b \in \mathbb{Z}, b \neq 0 \right\} \] Here, \( \mathbb{Z} \) represents the set of integers, and \( a \) and \( b \) are integers with \( b \neq 0 \).

\( \textbf{Irrational numbers} \): All the numbers that cannot be expressed as a fraction of two integers. They have infinite, non-repeating decimal expansions. E.g., \( \sqrt{2}, e, \pi. \)

\( \textbf{Real numbers} \ \mathbb{R} \): the union of both rational and irrational numbers. Real numbers can be thought of as all the points on the number line.

Example:

Given the set \( \{-90, -2.5, 0, 0.\overline{28}, \sqrt{3}, 7/4, \pi, 16 \} \), identify which numbers belong to the following number systems: natural numbers, whole numbers, integers, rational numbers, irrational numbers and real numbers.

Solution:

Natural numbers: 16

Whole numbers: 0, 16

Integers: -90, 0, 16

Rational numbers: -90, -2.5, 0, \( 0.\overline{28} \), 7/4, 16

Irrational numbers: \( \sqrt{3} , \pi \)

Real numbers: -90, -2.5, 0, \( 0.\overline{28} \), \( \sqrt{3}, 7/4, \pi \), 16

Squares and Cubes

Squares and Square Roots:

The $\textbf{square}$ of a number is the result of multiplying the number by itself. To square any number $x$ means to raise the number to the second power, i.e., $x^2 = x \times x$. E.g., $3^2 = 3 \times 3 = 9$.

A $\textbf{square root}$ of a number $y$ is a number $x$ such that $x^2 = y$. In other words, the number $x$'s square is $y$. Every positive number x has two square roots: $\sqrt{x}$, which is positive and called the principal square root, or simply "the square root", and $-\sqrt{x}$, which is negative. E.g., $4$ and $-4$ are square roots of 16 because $4^2 = (-4)^2 = 16$. The symbol "$\sqrt{\phantom{x}}$" is called a radical sign.

All numbers with square roots that are rational are called $\textbf{perfect squares}$, such as $0$, $4$, $\frac{4}{9}$.


Cubes and Cube Roots:

Prime factorization can be used to find square roots and cube roots.


Example 1:

Mercury, Venus, and Earth orbit the Sun approximately every 3, 7, and 12 months, respectively. If the three planets are currently aligned, in how many months will they align again?

Solution:

$3 = 3$

$7 = 7$

$12 = 2^2 \times 3$

The LCM of $3$, $7$, and $12$ is $2^2 \times 3 \times 7 = 84$. Therefore, in $84$ months they will align again.

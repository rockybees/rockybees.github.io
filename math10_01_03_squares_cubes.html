Squares and Cubes

Squares and Square Roots:

The $\textbf{square}$ of a number is the result of multiplying the number by itself. To square a number $x$ means to raise it to the power of two, written as $x^2 = x \times x$. E.g., $3^2 = 3 \times 3 = 9$.

A $\textbf{square root}$ of a number $a$ is a number $x$ such that $x^2 = a$. In other words, $x$ is a value whose square equals $a$.
Every positive number $a$ has two square roots:

  • $\sqrt{a}$, the principal (positive) square root, or commonly referred to as "the square root"
  • $-\sqrt{a}$, the negative square root.
E.g., $4$ and $-4$ are square roots of 16 because: $4^2 = (-4)^2 = 16$.
The symbol $\sqrt{\phantom{x}}$ is called a radical sign.

Numbers whose square roots are rational are called $\textbf{perfect squares}$, such as $0$, $4$, $\frac{4}{9}$. $\sqrt{25} = 5$.


Cubes and Cube Roots:

The $\textbf{cube}$ of a number is that number raised to the power of three, written as $x^3 = x \times x \times x$. E.g., $3^3 = 3 \times 3 \times 3 = 27$.

In the example above, $3$ is called the cube root of the number $27$. The symbol $\sqrt[3]{\phantom{x}}$ is called a radical sign, indicating cube roots. $\sqrt[3]{125} = 5$.

Prime factorization can be used to find square roots and cube roots.


Example 1:

Find the positive square root of 144.

Solution:

$144 = 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 3 \times 3$,

Therefore, $\sqrt{144} = 2 \times 2 \times 3 = 12$.
